The definition of a life coach is,
"A person who coaches and encourages clients on matters having to do with careers or personal
challenges."

What a life coach does:
• Get to know you, including your current situation, obstacles, and goals
• Help you assess your position in relation to the goals and aspirations you've outlined
• Assist you in mapping the steps between where you are and where you want to be
• Provide information and resources to support you on your journey
• Generate progress reports and updates that help you track your progress
• Keep you accountable by developing new habits and routines
• Deliver ongoing support to help ensure you meet your goals

Bottom line: A life coach provides clarity and actionable insights. They help you see your goals and take the right steps to achieve them.

---

What a life coach does in practice:
Let's say you want to write a book. A life coach will start by talking with you about your goals — what's the book about? When do you want to finish it? What are your expectations after you write it? You and your coach need to get on the same page about any objectives you have.

Once you have a firmly established goal, your life coach will help you step back and break it down — including how many pages you need to write each month and each week. You will discuss what milestones separate you from your main goal. Breaking the big goal into smaller, more digestible goals so that you have a roadmap for success.

Your life coach will also help you see what's preventing you from reaching each milestone or goal. Do you have time dedicated to writing your book each day? Is someone reviewing your work? By helping you identify what's holding you back, your life coach empowers you to move forward. Fix the things preventing you from achieving your goal and the rest is as easy as following the roadmap.

Finally, your life coach inspires accountability. They'll chat with you every week or even multiple times each week to make sure you're meeting your goals. If you aren't, why? If you are, they'll talk through your progress with you and make sure you're still clearly focused on the big objective ahead.

This style of coaching applies to everything from writing a book, to learning a new skill, to transitioning into a new job or promotion at work. The methodology is the same, but the steps and approach are different for every person.

When you ask "what can a life coach do for me?" you're really asking "how can this person teach me to reach my goals?"

## Types of Life Coaching (Categories)

**Career Coaching**
Clarify purpose, navigate career transitions, or prepare for promotions. Supports goal setting, values alignment, and stepping into fulfilling, purpose-driven work.

**Relationship Coaching**
Heal old wounds, improve communication, or build deeper connection personally or professionally.

**Personal Development Coaching**
Enhance self-awareness, break through limiting beliefs, and achieve personal goals.

**Health and Wellness Coaching**
Improve fitness, nutrition, energy, and emotional resilience.

**Financial Coaching**
Build a healthy money mindset and achieve long-term financial life goals.

**Life Planning Coaching**
Design a roadmap for a fulfilling life, aligned with your values and passions. Creating your ideal life vision for personal or professional life.

## App Routines — Extrapolated from the above

### 1. Onboarding / Get to Know You
- On first login, the app runs a structured intake: current situation, main life areas the user cares about, biggest obstacles, and one primary goal to start with
- Store answers as a user profile that informs all future interactions
- Don't skip this — a real coach never starts without it

### 2. Goal Setup
- For every goal the user adds: ask what it is, when they want it done, and what success looks like to them
- Require at least one milestone before a goal is considered "active"
- Goals without a deadline and at least one milestone are flagged as incomplete

### 3. Milestone & Roadmap Breakdown
- When a goal is set, the app prompts the user to break it into milestones
- Each milestone gets a target date and a weekly action (e.g. "write 3 pages per week")
- The app surfaces the next milestone clearly on the dashboard — not the end goal, the next step

### 4. Blocker Identification
- At each check-in, ask: "What's getting in your way this week?"
- Log blockers per goal/milestone
- If the same blocker appears twice, flag it and suggest addressing it before moving forward
- Blockers become part of the user's profile and inform future coaching nudges

### 5. Weekly Check-in
- Every week (configurable), the app initiates a check-in:
  - Did you hit last week's action?
  - What's the plan for this week?
  - Any blockers?
- Short, structured, not optional — this is the core accountability loop

### 6. Progress Tracking & Reporting
- Dashboard shows: goals, milestones hit vs. missed, streak, overall completion rate
- Monthly summary generated automatically: what was set, what was done, what slipped
- User can see trend over time — not just today's score

### 7. Accountability Nudges
- If a check-in is missed, send a reminder (HA notification)
- If a milestone deadline passes without completion, flag it and ask why
- If the user is consistently hitting goals, acknowledge it — positive reinforcement matters

### 8. Ongoing Support Loop
- After each check-in, the app gives one focused suggestion for the coming week based on the user's stated goal and last result
- Not generic advice — specific to the user's current milestone and blockers
- This is where the AI agent eventually plugs in

### 9. Reassessment Triggers
- If a goal is missed 3 times in a row, prompt a reassessment: is this still the right goal? Is the timeline realistic?
- Give the user a structured way to adjust without feeling like they failed
- Reframe: "updating the roadmap" not "giving up"

### 10. Habit & Routine Building
- Alongside goals, users can define daily/weekly habits tied to a goal
- App tracks habit completion as part of the weekly score
- Habits that stick for 30 days get flagged as established
